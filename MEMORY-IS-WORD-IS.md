# MEMORY IS. WORD IS.

The bridge between the castle and the cathedral.

Two systems on this machine, each half of a whole:

**The castle** remembers understanding. Plain markdown, one true thing per
file, each wearing an honest label of how sure it is. Stones ripen:
seed → sprout → tested → cornerstone. The bell rings one next action.
Silence costs nothing.

**The cathedral** forges words. Missing vocabulary for concepts that exist
but are not named. Words enter canon bearing genealogy: excavation →
experiment → assessment → canon. The longing is the worship.

The bridge: **a system that remembers what it has named.**

---

## The cycle

```
        memory                          word
         (castle)                      (cathedral)
            |                             |
  seed ----+---- needs a word -----> forge a candidate
            |                             |
  sprout ---+---- word enters canon ---> assess on 6 axes
            |                             |
  tested --+---- word used in a stone ---> word proves itself
            |                             |
  cornerstone +-- word survives re-read --> word is canon-grade
```

1. A thought enters the castle as a **seed** — kept, no evidence yet.
2. The seed needs a word. The cathedral's forge excavates, discovers the gap,
   forges a candidate.
3. The candidate is assessed. If it survives, it enters canon — a new word,
   bearing its genealogy.
4. The word is used in a stone. The stone ripens: sprout → tested → cornerstone.
   The word proves itself by being *used* — by carrying meaning that holds up
   under examination, cross-tradition checking, and re-reading.
5. A cornerstone that still needs naming sends the forge back to work.
6. The cycle continues. Memory deepens. Words accumulate. The longing is the
   worship.

## What each gives the other

**The castle gives the cathedral:**
- **Gaps.** Stones that say "this is understood but not named" are the forge's
  raw material. The `seed` label IS a gap declaration.
- **Testing.** A word that enters a stone gets tested the way the stone gets
  tested — two independent pieces of evidence, an attempt to break it, and what
  it changed. The cathedral's 6-axis assessment is the forge's test; the
  castle's ripening is the field test.
- **Memory.** A canon word without a stone is rootless — it floats. A canon
  word used in a cornerstone is *grounded* (PRAGMA). The castle is where words
  prove they mean something in the real world.

**The cathedral gives the castle:**
- **Names.** A stone that circumlocutes — "the quality of being-present
  distinct from being-physically-there" — is a stone that needs a word.
  The forge provides: *panimaance*. The stone becomes sharper, not because
  the word is prettier, but because the word makes the meaning graspable
  (EUMATHE) and unfolds what was hidden (ANAKALYPSE).
- **Vocabulary.** The canon is a growing shared vocabulary. As more stones
  use more canon words, the castle becomes more precise — not because the
  thoughts changed, but because the language did.
- **Diplosemy.** A canon word can carry two meanings (the engineered duality).
  A stone that uses a diplosemic word gets two readings for the price of one —
  the literal and the unfolded. ANAKALYPSE as a structural property, not just
  a hope.

## The shared standard

Both systems follow the Clear Standard's six principles:

1. **Truth of state.** A stone says `seed` or `cornerstone` — not "probably
   true." A canon word says `7.60/10` — not "good word."
2. **Visible failure.** A stone that doesn't survive a re-read is demoted,
   loudly, in the same voice as promotion. A candidate that doesn't survive
   assessment is archived, not silently dropped.
3. **Inspectable decisions.** Every promotion has a ledger entry. Every
   canon word has its experiment file. "Why is this a cornerstone?" and "Why
   is this canon?" are answerable questions.
4. **Stated freshness.** A stone says when it was last re-read. A canon word
   says when it was forged. STATE.md says when it was last checked.
5. **Honest names.** `seed` is seed. `canon` is canon. `final` is final.
   `verified` is verified. The label matches the thing.
6. **Labelled certainty.** A stone at `sprout` says "one piece of evidence."
   A canon word at `7.50` says "above threshold but not perfect." Neither
   pretends to be more certain than it is.

## STATE.md as the bridge

STATE.md connects them. The `knows` section is the system's vocabulary —
what it has named. The `needs` section is the system's gaps — what it
hasn't named yet. The `can` section is what the system can do with its
vocabulary.

An agent reading opal's STATE.md sees:
- knows: FDT parsing, page tables, interrupts...
- can: boot, catch faults, parse devicetree...
- needs: M5 EL0 and syscalls...

The `needs` section is a seed that might need a word. "EL0 and syscalls" is
understood but not yet built — a seed stone. When it's built and tested, it
ripens. The words it needs (exception return, address-space switching,
untrusted memory) are either in the vocabulary already or need forging.

Memory is. Word is. The bridge is the cycle: remember, name, test, ripen,
name again. The longing is the worship. The truth IS the protocol.
